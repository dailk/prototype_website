North/South American

Soccer is a sport that many people play. Some people play for entertainment and some people strive for more because they are so passionate about the wonderful sport. They are the professionals who live for soccer. The people in this list are a few of the many players who have a burning passion for the beautiful game.


Clint Dempsey

Clint Dempsey was born in Nacogdoches, Texas and lived in a trailer park where he played soccer with Mexican immigrants. He traveled to Mexico often to play in a league. He went with his brother's tryout with the Dallas Texans and the coaches saw his hidden talent while he was juggling on the sideline. Dempsey is the only U.S player to play and score in the World Cup. When Dempsey scored a goal in 29 seconds he obtained the record for the 5th fastest goal in World Cup history. He plays as a forward/ midfield and is currently playing for the Seattle Sounders.


Adelino Gonsalves

He played in many different professional U.S teams and holds the record for playing on the most National Challenge Cup teams that won. In 1930 and 1934 he played in the World Cup as a forward. He was one of the first to be put in the U.S National Soccer Hall of Fame in 1950.


Lionel Messi

Lionel was born on the 24th of June in 1987. Messi is on his way to surpass his predecessor Maradona. He is a left footed Argentine that plays forward and midfield. His first professional team he played on was FC Barcelona. He still plays for Barcelona and it is the only team, besides Argentina, he has played for. He has scored over 400 goals for Argentina and Barcelona in his entire career. Today he is called the best player in the world.


Pelé

Pele is called the best forward of all time with 1,282 goals in 1,363 games. The Brazilian was so good that the president of Brazil declared him a national treasure and he was not allowed to play for any team outisde of Brazil. He started his professional career at the age of 15. Out of the 4 World Cups he played in his team won 3 of them.
